Full-Service Moving vs. Labor-Only: Which Is Right for You?

February 5, 2026 7 min read Sutton Movers Team

Planning a move in Southern California? One of the first decisions you will face is whether to hire a full-service moving company or book labor-only help. Both options have clear advantages depending on your budget, timeline, and how much control you want over the process. This guide breaks down everything you need to know so you can choose with confidence.

What Is Full-Service Moving?

A full-service move means the moving company handles the entire process from start to finish. The crew arrives with a truck, loads your furniture and boxes, drives everything to your new home, and unloads it all at the destination. Many full-service packages also include packing and unpacking, furniture disassembly and reassembly, and protective wrapping for fragile items.

With full-service moving, you are essentially handing off the logistics to professionals. You do not need to rent a truck, buy moving blankets, or recruit friends to help carry your couch down three flights of stairs. The moving company coordinates the timeline, provides all the equipment, and manages every detail of the physical relocation.

Pros of Full-Service Moving

  • Convenience: Everything is handled for you, from packing tape to transportation.
  • Speed: Experienced crews can load and deliver a full household in a single day.
  • Protection: Professional movers use blankets, straps, and padding to minimize damage risk.
  • Less physical strain: No heavy lifting on your part means fewer injuries and less exhaustion.
  • Insurance coverage: Reputable companies carry liability insurance for your belongings during transit.

Cons of Full-Service Moving

  • Higher cost: You are paying for labor, the truck, fuel, equipment, and insurance all in one package.
  • Less control: You are trusting someone else with your schedule, route, and handling of your items.
  • Availability: Peak-season weekends can book up quickly, limiting your date flexibility.

What Is Labor-Only Moving?

Labor-only moving means you hire professional movers strictly for their physical help. They load and unload your belongings, but you provide the truck, trailer, or portable storage container yourself. This option is popular with customers who rent a U-Haul, use a PODS container, or borrow a friend's truck but still want trained movers to handle the heavy lifting.

Labor-only crews bring the same expertise and care as full-service movers. They know how to navigate tight staircases, protect doorframes, and stack boxes efficiently inside a truck. The difference is simply that transportation is your responsibility.

Pros of Labor-Only Moving

  • Lower cost: You pay only for the labor hours, not the truck or fuel.
  • Flexibility: Load on your schedule and drive at your own pace.
  • Control over the vehicle: Choose the truck size, rental company, and route that work best for you.
  • Great for partial moves: Ideal if you only need help with heavy items like furniture or appliances.
  • Container-friendly: Perfect for PODS, U-Pack, or other portable storage solutions.

Cons of Labor-Only Moving

  • More coordination: You manage the truck rental, driving, parking, and return separately.
  • Driving responsibility: If you are not comfortable driving a large rental truck, this can be stressful.
  • Limited insurance: The moving labor covers handling, but transit insurance depends on your rental or container provider.

When Should You Choose Full-Service?

Full-Service Is Ideal When...

  • You are relocating a 3+ bedroom home with lots of furniture
  • You have a tight timeline and need everything done in one day
  • You are moving long distance and do not want to drive a truck across state lines
  • You have mobility limitations or health concerns that prevent heavy lifting
  • You want a single point of contact managing the entire move

Labor-Only Is Ideal When...

  • You already have a rental truck, trailer, or PODS container
  • You are moving a studio or one-bedroom apartment
  • You want to save money and do not mind driving the truck yourself
  • You only need help loading or unloading, not both
  • You are doing a partial move, like delivering furniture from a storage unit

Cost Breakdown: What to Expect

Cost is often the deciding factor between these two options. A full-service local move for a two-bedroom home in the San Diego area typically runs between $1,000 and $2,000 depending on distance, stairs, and the amount of furniture. This price includes the truck, fuel, equipment, and a crew of two to three movers for four to six hours.

A labor-only booking for the same size move usually costs between $300 and $700, covering two to three movers for three to five hours of loading or unloading. Add your truck rental cost of $50 to $150, and you are looking at a total somewhere between $350 and $850. The savings can be substantial, especially for shorter, local moves.

Pro Tip If you are using a portable container like PODS or U-Pack for a long-distance move, labor-only is almost always the smarter choice. You get the benefit of professional loading without paying for a truck you do not need. The container company handles the driving, and you only pay movers for the hours they work at each end.

Quick Decision Checklist

Still not sure? Run through these questions:

  1. Do you already have a truck or container? If yes, labor-only saves you money.
  2. Are you comfortable driving a large vehicle? If not, go full-service.
  3. Is your budget tight? Labor-only typically costs 50-60% less than full-service.
  4. Do you need packing and disassembly help? Full-service often includes these extras.
  5. Are you moving more than 50 miles? Full-service eliminates the hassle of a long truck drive.
  6. Do you only need help at one end? Labor-only lets you book just a loading or unloading crew.

The right choice depends entirely on your circumstances. There is no one-size-fits-all answer, but with the information above, you should have a clear picture of which option matches your priorities, whether that is maximum convenience or maximum savings.
