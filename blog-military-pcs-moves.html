Why Military Families Choose Sutton Movers for PCS Moves

March 1, 2026 7 min read

It means uprooting your household, coordinating logistics under tight deadlines, and doing it all while managing the demands of military life. At Sutton Movers, we understand that PCS moves are not just another relocation. They come with unique pressures, strict timelines, and government requirements that civilian moving companies often overlook.

Understanding PCS Moves and Your Options

When you receive PCS orders, you typically have several options for how your household goods will be transported. Understanding these options is the first step toward a smooth transition.

  • Government-Arranged Move (GHC): The military contracts a moving company through the Global Household Goods Contract. While this option is fully covered, you have limited control over scheduling and which company handles your belongings.
  • Personally Procured Move (PPM / DITY): Formerly known as a Do-It-Yourself (DITY) move, this option lets you arrange your own transportation and receive a reimbursement based on what the government would have paid. Many families choose this route because it gives them more control and can result in savings you keep as profit.
  • Partial PPM: A hybrid approach where the military ships some items and you handle the rest. This works well when you want certain valuables or essentials transported separately under your supervision.

Sutton Movers is a trusted partner for PPM and partial PPM moves. We provide the labor, packing, and transportation you need while you retain the financial benefit of managing your own relocation. We also supply weight tickets and documentation to help you file for reimbursement without headaches.

Tips for a Smooth Military Relocation

After helping hundreds of military families relocate across Southern California, we have learned a few things about what makes PCS moves go smoothly. Here are our top recommendations.

  • Start early. As soon as you receive orders, begin planning. Book your movers, arrange temporary housing if needed, and start decluttering. The earlier you begin, the more options you have.
  • Get multiple weight tickets. For PPM moves, you need certified weight tickets showing your vehicle or truck empty and loaded. We can help coordinate this process so your reimbursement paperwork is accurate.
  • Document everything. Photograph your belongings before packing, especially electronics, furniture, and valuables. This creates a clear record in case you need to file a claim.
  • Keep essentials separate. Pack a separate bag or box with uniforms, important documents (orders, medical records, school records), medications, chargers, and a few days of clothing. This bag stays with you, not on the truck.
  • Coordinate with your housing office. Schedule your move-out inspection early and understand what cleaning or repairs are expected. This prevents delays and charges on your final day.
  • Know your allowances. Review your PCS entitlements on the Defense Finance and Accounting Service website so you understand your weight limits, per diem rates, and reimbursement timelines.
